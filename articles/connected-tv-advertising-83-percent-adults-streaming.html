Connected TV Advertising: 83% of Adults Stream Content

With 83% of adults using streaming services versus 36% cable subscribers, connected TV presents major advertising opportunities. Businesses can capitalize on this shift toward streaming-based marketing.

By AIEarn Editorial2026-05-09Neil Patel

The television advertising landscape has undergone a seismic shift that savvy entrepreneurs and marketers can no longer ignore. With 83% of adults now streaming content compared to just 36% who maintain cable subscriptions, connected TV (CTV) advertising represents one of the most lucrative opportunities in digital marketing today. This dramatic reversal from traditional broadcasting creates unprecedented possibilities for businesses to reach engaged audiences while leveraging AI-powered tools to maximize their advertising returns.

The migration from cable to streaming isn't just changing how we consume entertainment—it's revolutionizing how money flows through the advertising ecosystem. Connected TV advertising spending reached $21.2 billion in 2023, with projections showing continued double-digit growth through 2025. For entrepreneurs and businesses looking to capitalize on this trend, the timing couldn't be better to establish a foothold in this expanding market.

The Connected TV Gold Rush: Understanding the Opportunity

Connected TV advertising operates differently from traditional television, offering precision targeting capabilities that were impossible with broadcast media. Unlike cable TV's broad demographic targeting, CTV platforms can identify specific households, track viewing behaviors, and measure actual engagement—creating a data-rich environment where businesses can make money with AI-driven optimization strategies.

The economics are compelling. While traditional TV advertising required minimum spends often exceeding $50,000 for local markets, connected TV campaigns can start as low as $1,000 per month on platforms like Samsung Ads or $5,000 on premium networks through programmatic buying. This lower barrier to entry means smaller businesses and individual entrepreneurs can compete alongside major brands for viewer attention.

The audience quality on streaming platforms also surpasses traditional cable in key metrics that matter for revenue generation. CTV viewers are 85% more likely to engage with ads, and the average household income of streaming subscribers is $75,000—significantly higher than the $58,000 average for cable households. This demographic shift means advertising dollars go further in reaching consumers with actual purchasing power.

Three Proven Strategies to Profit from Connected TV Advertising

Strategy 1: Launch a CTV Advertising Agency
The supply-demand imbalance in connected TV expertise creates substantial agency opportunities. Businesses desperately need CTV advertising help, but few agencies offer comprehensive streaming advertising services. Start by mastering one platform like The Trade Desk (minimum $20,000 account) or more accessible options like StackAdapt ($5,000 minimum). Focus on specific niches—local restaurants, e-commerce brands, or professional services—where you can demonstrate clear ROI. Agencies typically charge 15-20% management fees plus markup on media spend, meaning a client spending $10,000 monthly generates $1,500-2,000 in recurring revenue.

Strategy 2: Create and Monetize Streaming Content
The explosion in streaming platforms has created insatiable demand for content, opening revenue streams for creators willing to think strategically. Platforms like Tubi, Crackle, and Pluto TV actively seek content providers and offer revenue-sharing agreements. Produce niche content targeting specific demographics—cooking shows for millennials, financial advice for retirees, or tech tutorials for small businesses. Use AI tools like Synthesia for cost-effective video production or Jasper for scriptwriting. Content creators earn $2-8 per 1,000 views on ad-supported platforms, with successful channels generating $5,000-50,000 monthly.

Strategy 3: Become a CTV Data and Analytics Consultant
Connected TV generates massive amounts of viewer data, but most businesses lack expertise to interpret and act on these insights. Position yourself as a CTV analytics specialist using tools like Nielsen's Advanced TV solutions or more affordable options like TVision ($500/month for small businesses). Help companies optimize their streaming advertising campaigns, identify high-value audience segments, and improve creative performance. Analytics consultants charge $150-300 per hour, with retainer clients paying $3,000-10,000 monthly for ongoing optimization services.

Maximizing Profits with AI-Powered CTV Tools

The intersection of artificial intelligence and connected TV advertising creates unique opportunities to make money with AI while delivering superior results for clients or your own campaigns. Platforms like VideoAmp use machine learning to optimize cross-screen campaigns, while tools like Innovid's DCO (Dynamic Creative Optimization) automatically customize ads based on viewer preferences and behaviors.

AI-powered creative tools are particularly valuable for CTV success. Platforms like Waymark enable users to create professional-quality TV commercials in minutes rather than weeks, reducing production costs from $10,000-50,000 to under $500 per ad. These efficiency gains allow entrepreneurs to offer competitive pricing while maintaining healthy profit margins.

Attribution and measurement AI tools like iSpot.tv or TVSquared help businesses prove advertising ROI—a critical capability for retaining clients and justifying rate increases. Companies using AI-driven CTV optimization typically see 30-50% better performance than those relying on manual campaign management.

Building Long-Term Wealth in the Streaming Economy

The shift toward connected TV represents more than a temporary trend—it's a fundamental restructuring of the entertainment and advertising industries. Early movers who establish expertise, relationships, and systems in CTV advertising will benefit from years of sustained growth as cable continues its decline.

Success requires staying ahead of platform changes, privacy regulations, and measurement standards. Invest in continuous learning through industry certifications from The Trade Desk, Google's DV360, or Amazon DSP. Build relationships with streaming platform representatives, content creators, and other industry professionals who can provide deal flow and partnership opportunities.

The connected TV advertising opportunity won't remain accessible to small players indefinitely. As major agencies and tech companies recognize the profit potential, competition will intensify and barriers to entry will rise. The entrepreneurs who act now—while the market remains fragmented and clients still need education about CTV benefits—will establish the strongest positions for long-term wealth creation.
