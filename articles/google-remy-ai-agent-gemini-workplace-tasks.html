Google Tests Remy AI Agent for Workplace Tasks

Google internally tests Remy, an AI personal agent for Gemini that handles work tasks. This points to emerging opportunities in AI agent development and workplace automation services.

By AIEarn Editorial2026-05-09AI News

The workplace AI revolution just got more personal. Google's internal testing of Remy, an AI agent designed to handle complex workplace tasks through their Gemini platform, signals a massive shift toward autonomous digital assistants that could fundamentally change how we approach productivity—and create entirely new revenue streams for savvy entrepreneurs and professionals.

While tech giants battle for AI supremacy, the real opportunity lies in understanding how these tools will reshape the service economy and position yourself to capitalize on the transition.

The AI Agent Economy Takes Shape

Remy represents Google's ambitious push into what industry analysts call "agentic AI"—systems that don't just respond to prompts but actively complete multi-step tasks with minimal human oversight. Unlike ChatGPT or current Gemini implementations that require constant guidance, Remy is designed to handle complex workflows like scheduling meetings, managing email campaigns, and coordinating project deliverables autonomously.

This development matters because it validates a market that PwC estimates will reach $15.7 trillion by 2030. But here's the crucial insight: the money won't primarily flow to AI companies—it'll flow to the businesses and individuals who best leverage these tools to deliver superior services at scale.

Early reports suggest Remy can maintain context across multiple applications, remember preferences, and even learn from past decisions. This capability gap between current AI tools and true agents creates a narrow but lucrative window for early adopters to establish competitive advantages before the technology becomes commoditized.

Three Immediate Money-Making Opportunities

1. Launch AI-Powered Virtual Assistant Services
The emergence of sophisticated AI agents like Remy creates demand for human oversight and customization. Start a virtual assistant service that combines AI efficiency with human judgment. Target small business owners who need administrative support but can't afford full-time employees. Charge $25-50 per hour for services that blend AI automation with strategic oversight.

Tools like Make.com ($9/month) or Zapier ($20/month) can help you create workflows today that mirror what Remy promises. Focus on email management, appointment scheduling, and basic content creation. As AI agents become more sophisticated, your role evolves from doing tasks to managing AI systems—a higher-value position.

2. Develop AI Agent Training and Implementation Consulting
Businesses will need help configuring and optimizing AI agents for their specific workflows. Position yourself as an AI implementation consultant by mastering current tools and staying ahead of developments like Remy. Start with local businesses, charging $150-300 per hour for setup and training services.

Begin building expertise with accessible tools: Claude for complex reasoning tasks ($20/month), Notion AI for document management ($10/month), and Calendly's AI features for scheduling automation. Document case studies and results to build credibility before enterprise-level AI agents launch widely.

3. Create AI-Enhanced Digital Products
Use current AI capabilities to create scalable digital products that solve specific workplace problems. Develop email templates, workflow optimization guides, or productivity courses enhanced by AI insights. The key is creating products that demonstrate AI's potential while remaining valuable as the technology evolves.

Consider launching a subscription service ($29-99/month) that provides AI-generated business documents, marketing materials, or operational templates. Use tools like Jasper ($49/month) or Copy.ai ($36/month) to create the content, but focus on curation, customization, and strategic application—the human elements that remain valuable regardless of AI advances.

Strategic Positioning for the Agent Revolution

The transition to AI agents won't happen overnight, creating opportunities for those who can bridge the gap between today's tools and tomorrow's capabilities. Smart money focuses on building skills and services that become more valuable as AI improves, not less.

Focus on developing expertise in AI prompt engineering, workflow optimization, and strategic automation. These skills translate directly to managing more sophisticated agents like Remy when they become widely available. Companies will pay premium rates for professionals who can maximize AI ROI while maintaining quality control.

Consider the broader implications: if Remy can handle routine workplace tasks, human value shifts toward strategy, creativity, and relationship management. Position your services and skills accordingly. The businesses that thrive will be those that use AI agents to scale human expertise, not replace human judgment.

Preparing for the Post-Agent Workplace

Google's Remy testing suggests sophisticated AI agents will reach mainstream adoption within 18-24 months. This timeline provides a crucial opportunity to establish market position before competition intensifies. The key is building systems and expertise that scale with improving AI capabilities rather than being disrupted by them.

Start experimenting with current AI tools to understand their limitations and strengths. Document what works, what doesn't, and where human oversight remains essential. This knowledge becomes invaluable when advising others or building AI-enhanced services.

The businesses that will make money with AI aren't necessarily those with the best technology—they're those with the best understanding of how to apply technology to solve real problems. Remy and similar AI agents are tools, not strategies. Success comes from wielding these tools more effectively than competitors.
