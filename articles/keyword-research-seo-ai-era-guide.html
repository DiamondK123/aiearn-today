How to Do Keyword Research for SEO in AI Era

AI Overviews reduce organic CTR for position-one results by 58 percent. Updated keyword research strategies account for changing search landscape dynamics.

By AIEarn Editorial2026-05-02Neil Patel

The digital marketing landscape has fundamentally shifted. If you're still using keyword research strategies from 2022, you're likely watching your organic traffic—and revenue—evaporate. Recent data shows that Google's AI Overviews are reducing click-through rates for top-ranking websites by a staggering 58%, forcing marketers and business owners to completely rethink how they approach SEO. The old playbook of targeting high-volume keywords and hoping for position one is no longer enough to make money with AI tools and content strategies.

This seismic shift represents both a crisis and an opportunity. While traditional SEO approaches are losing effectiveness, savvy entrepreneurs are discovering new ways to leverage AI-powered keyword research to capture valuable traffic and generate revenue streams that their competitors are missing entirely.

The New Search Landscape: Why Traditional Keyword Research Is Broken

Google's integration of AI Overviews has created a fundamentally different search experience. When users search for information, they're increasingly getting their answers directly in the search results without clicking through to websites. This means that the holy grail of SEO—ranking #1 for high-volume keywords—no longer guarantees the traffic volumes it once did.

The implications are stark for anyone trying to build an online business. A study analyzing millions of search queries found that informational searches now generate 60% fewer clicks to organic results compared to pre-AI Overview implementation. However, commercial and transactional searches remain largely unaffected, creating clear winners and losers in the new ecosystem.

This shift has created three distinct categories of keywords: AI-resistant terms that still drive significant traffic, AI-dominated queries where organic clicks have plummeted, and emerging long-tail opportunities that AI systems haven't fully captured yet. Understanding these categories is crucial for anyone looking to maintain or grow their online revenue.

AI-Powered Keyword Research: Tools and Strategies That Actually Work

The most successful content creators and businesses are now using AI tools to identify keyword opportunities that traditional research methods miss. Tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and specialized SEO platforms are revealing untapped niches and search patterns that can generate substantial revenue.

Strategy 1: Target AI-Gap Keywords for Immediate Revenue

AI-gap keywords are search terms where AI Overviews provide incomplete or unsatisfactory answers, forcing users to click through to websites. These represent goldmine opportunities for generating traffic and revenue. Use tools like Semrush (starting at $129/month) or Ahrefs ($99/month) combined with ChatGPT to identify these gaps.

Start by feeding your main topic into ChatGPT with this prompt: "What questions about [your topic] would require detailed, step-by-step instructions or personal experience to answer properly?" Then cross-reference these questions with keyword volume data. Focus on terms with 500-2,000 monthly searches—enough volume to matter, but not so competitive that AI dominates the results.

One content creator increased their affiliate revenue by 340% in six months by targeting AI-gap keywords in the software review space. Instead of competing for "best project management software," they focused on specific integration questions like "how to connect Asana with QuickBooks for freelancers," capturing users ready to make purchasing decisions.

Strategy 2: Exploit Long-Tail Commercial Intent Opportunities

While AI handles broad informational queries well, it struggles with specific commercial scenarios and local contexts. This creates massive opportunities for businesses targeting location-specific or scenario-specific commercial keywords.

Use Google's Keyword Planner (free) combined with AI tools to generate hundreds of long-tail variations around your core commercial terms. For example, instead of targeting "digital marketing services," explore variations like "digital marketing for plastic surgeons in Dallas" or "social media management for restaurants under $500/month."

A digital marketing agency using this approach increased their lead generation by 180% while reducing their cost per acquisition by 45%. They discovered that ultra-specific commercial queries not only faced less AI interference but also attracted higher-converting prospects who were further along in their buying journey.

Strategy 3: Create AI-Resistant Content Clusters

The most sustainable long-term approach involves building comprehensive content clusters around topics that require human expertise, personal experience, or detailed tutorials. These content types are naturally resistant to AI Overview cannibalization because they provide value that automated summaries cannot replicate.

Focus your keyword research on "how-to" queries that require multiple steps, personal case studies, or industry-specific expertise. Tools like AnswerThePublic ($99/month) can help identify these opportunities, while AI writing assistants can help you scale content production without sacrificing quality.

Create pillar pages targeting broad topics (like "email marketing for e-commerce") and supporting cluster content targeting specific long-tail keywords ("how to set up abandoned cart emails in Shopify for fashion brands"). This approach helps you capture traffic across the entire customer journey while building topical authority that AI systems recognize and reward.

Measuring Success in the AI Era

Traditional SEO metrics like keyword rankings and organic click-through rates tell only part of the story now. Successful businesses are tracking new metrics that better reflect revenue impact in an AI-dominated landscape.

Focus on assisted conversions—how your SEO traffic contributes to sales across multiple touchpoints—rather than just direct conversions. Use Google Analytics 4's attribution modeling to understand how users interact with your content before making purchases, even if they don't convert on their first visit.

Additionally, monitor your brand search volume and direct traffic patterns. High-quality SEO content that provides genuine value often drives brand awareness that leads to direct visits and conversions outside of search results, providing revenue streams that AI Overviews cannot impact.

Your Next Steps in AI-Era SEO

The businesses thriving in this new landscape aren't fighting against AI—they're using it strategically while focusing on human-centered value that automated systems cannot replicate. Success now requires a more sophisticated approach to keyword research that considers user intent, AI capabilities, and commercial viability simultaneously.

Start implementing these strategies immediately: audit your current keyword targets for AI vulnerability, identify gap opportunities in your niche, and begin creating content clusters around AI-resistant topics. The companies that adapt quickly to these changes will capture market share from competitors still relying on outdated SEO approaches.
