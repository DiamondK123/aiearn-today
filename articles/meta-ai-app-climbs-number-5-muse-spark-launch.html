Meta AI App Hits #5 After Muse Spark Launch

Meta's new proprietary AI model drives massive app adoption, jumping from #57 to #5 on App Store. Marks shift away from open-source Llama strategy.

By AIEarn Editorial2026-04-11TechCrunch

Meta's strategic pivot from open-source AI development to proprietary models just delivered a masterclass in market positioning. The launch of Muse Spark, Meta's new closed-source AI model, propelled their standalone AI app from a modest #57 to the coveted #5 spot on the App Store rankings. This dramatic surge signals more than just successful product marketing—it reveals shifting opportunities for entrepreneurs and professionals looking to make money with AI tools in an increasingly competitive landscape.

The meteoric rise of Meta's AI app represents a fundamental shift in the company's approach to artificial intelligence. After years of championing open-source development with their Llama models, Meta has embraced a more traditional tech giant strategy: keeping their most advanced capabilities proprietary. This transition mirrors moves by OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic, who have built massive valuations around closed AI systems. The App Store ranking jump from #57 to #5 translates to millions of new users and positions Meta as a serious competitor in the consumer AI space.

What Muse Spark's Success Means for AI Entrepreneurs

The rapid adoption of Muse Spark offers critical insights for anyone building AI-powered businesses or services. Meta's success demonstrates that consumers are actively seeking AI tools that deliver immediate, tangible value rather than just novelty features. Early user reports suggest Muse Spark excels at creative tasks, code generation, and complex reasoning—capabilities that translate directly into revenue opportunities for savvy entrepreneurs.

This market validation creates a ripple effect across the AI ecosystem. As major platforms like Meta invest heavily in consumer-facing AI applications, they're essentially educating the market and creating demand for complementary services, specialized tools, and AI-enhanced workflows that smaller businesses can capitalize on.

Three Actionable Strategies to Monetize the AI App Boom

1. Become an AI Integration Consultant
With Meta AI joining ChatGPT, Claude, and other major platforms in mainstream adoption, businesses desperately need guidance on which tools to use and how to integrate them effectively. Start by mastering 3-4 leading AI platforms (including Meta AI) and offer consulting services at $75-150 per hour. Focus on specific industries like real estate, marketing agencies, or small law firms where AI adoption is accelerating but expertise is scarce. You can begin by offering free workshops to local business groups and converting attendees into paying clients.

2. Create AI-Enhanced Service Offerings
Use the capabilities demonstrated by Muse Spark—creative content generation, code assistance, and analytical reasoning—to enhance existing service businesses. For example, if you're a freelance writer, leverage Meta AI and similar tools to offer faster turnaround times and expand into new content types like technical documentation or creative campaigns. This allows you to increase your effective hourly rate from $25-50 to $75-100 while actually working fewer manual hours. The key is positioning AI as a capability enhancer, not a replacement for human expertise.

3. Develop AI-First Digital Products
The surge in AI app adoption indicates strong consumer willingness to pay for AI-powered solutions. Consider creating digital products that combine multiple AI platforms for specific use cases. For instance, develop a course on "AI-Powered Content Creation" that teaches students to use Meta AI for ideation, ChatGPT for writing, and Midjourney for visuals. Price these comprehensive courses at $297-497, targeting professionals who want to streamline their workflows but lack the time to research tools individually.

Platform Strategy Implications

Meta's shift from open-source to proprietary AI models creates both opportunities and challenges for developers and businesses. The company's decision to keep Muse Spark capabilities exclusive means users must engage directly with Meta's ecosystem, potentially limiting third-party integrations that made Llama attractive to developers. However, this also creates opportunities for businesses that can effectively utilize Meta's AI through their official channels and APIs.

The App Store success suggests Meta will likely expand Muse Spark's capabilities and potentially introduce premium tiers or API access for businesses. Early adopters who establish expertise with Meta AI now will be positioned advantageously when these commercial opportunities emerge. Given Meta's track record with business tools like Facebook Ads Manager and Instagram for Business, expect sophisticated monetization features to follow.

Market Positioning and Competitive Dynamics

The rapid climb to #5 on the App Store puts Meta AI in direct competition with established players like ChatGPT, which has maintained strong rankings since its mobile launch. This competition benefits users through improved features and capabilities, but it also creates urgency for businesses to diversify their AI tool stack. Relying on a single AI platform becomes increasingly risky as companies pivot strategies and change pricing models.

Smart entrepreneurs should view this competitive landscape as validation of the AI market's sustainability rather than a threat. When tech giants invest billions in consumer AI adoption, they're essentially subsidizing market education and creating demand that smaller, specialized businesses can serve more effectively than large platforms.

Meta's Muse Spark launch and subsequent App Store success represents a maturation of the consumer AI market. The opportunity for individuals and small businesses to make money with AI tools has never been stronger, but success requires strategic thinking about platform dependencies, skill development, and market positioning. Whether you choose to become an AI consultant, enhance existing services, or create new digital products, the key is acting quickly while the market is still establishing new norms and consumer expectations.
