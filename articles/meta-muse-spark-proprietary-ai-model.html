Meta Launches Muse Spark: New Proprietary AI Model Beats Llama

Meta abandons open-source strategy with first proprietary model since Superintelligence Labs formation. App jumps to #5 on App Store after launch.

By AIEarn Editorial2026-04-11VentureBeat

Meta's latest move signals a seismic shift in the AI landscape—and potentially new opportunities for entrepreneurs looking to make money with AI. The tech giant's surprise launch of Muse Spark, their first proprietary AI model since forming the Superintelligence Labs division, marks a dramatic departure from their open-source Llama strategy. Within hours of its debut, the accompanying app rocketed to #5 on the App Store, suggesting Meta has struck gold with a model that reportedly outperforms their widely-adopted Llama series.

This strategic pivot raises critical questions for AI entrepreneurs, developers, and businesses who have built their revenue streams around Meta's previously open ecosystem. More importantly, it creates new monetization opportunities for those quick enough to adapt to this changing landscape.

Why Meta Abandoned Open Source—And What It Means for Your Bottom Line

Meta's shift from open-source to proprietary represents more than just a change in philosophy—it's a calculated business decision that could reshape how entrepreneurs make money with AI. The company's Llama models, while revolutionary for democratizing AI access, generated limited direct revenue for Meta. By contrast, proprietary models allow for subscription tiers, premium features, and controlled access that translate directly to profit.

For AI entrepreneurs, this change presents both challenges and opportunities. Those who built businesses entirely dependent on free Llama access may face future costs, but early adopters of Muse Spark could gain competitive advantages through access to superior AI capabilities. The app's rapid climb to #5 on the App Store demonstrates significant market demand for Meta's new approach.

The formation of Meta's Superintelligence Labs division, which produced Muse Spark, signals the company's commitment to competing directly with OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google in the premium AI space. This competition typically drives innovation and creates new niches for savvy entrepreneurs to exploit.

Three Actionable Strategies to Profit from Meta's AI Shift

1. Become an Early Muse Spark Integration Specialist
With Muse Spark's superior performance over Llama, businesses will need help integrating this new technology. Position yourself as a specialist in Muse Spark implementation. Start by downloading the app, experimenting with its capabilities, and documenting use cases in specific industries. Freelancers on platforms like Upwork are already charging $75-150 per hour for AI integration services. Early expertise in Muse Spark could command premium rates of $150-250 per hour.

2. Develop Muse Spark-Powered SaaS Solutions
The app's App Store success indicates strong consumer appeal, but B2B opportunities remain largely untapped. Consider building specialized tools that leverage Muse Spark's advanced capabilities for specific industries. For example, a Muse Spark-powered content optimization tool for e-commerce could command $49-199 monthly subscriptions. Research shows AI-powered SaaS tools typically achieve 15-20% monthly revenue growth when targeting underserved niches.

3. Create Educational Content and Courses
Meta's strategy shift creates immediate demand for educational resources about Muse Spark. Develop comprehensive courses comparing Muse Spark to Llama, showcasing practical applications, and teaching integration techniques. Online course creators in the AI space report average earnings of $3,000-15,000 per course, with top performers earning six figures annually. Focus on practical, hands-on tutorials rather than theoretical content to maximize enrollment and completion rates.

Strategic Considerations for Long-Term Success

While Muse Spark presents immediate opportunities, successful AI entrepreneurs must consider Meta's long-term trajectory. The company's willingness to abandon open-source suggests future models may follow similar proprietary patterns. This shift could mean subscription costs for access, but also more stable, enterprise-grade service levels compared to free alternatives.

Smart entrepreneurs should diversify their AI tool dependencies rather than relying solely on any single provider. Consider building systems that can work with multiple AI models—Muse Spark, GPT-4, Claude, and others—to avoid vendor lock-in and maintain competitive flexibility.

The rapid app success also indicates Meta's improved ability to market AI products directly to consumers. This suggests opportunities for affiliate marketing, partnership programs, and complementary tool development may emerge as Meta expands Muse Spark's ecosystem.

Market Impact and Revenue Projections

Industry analysts predict Meta's proprietary AI strategy could generate $2-5 billion in additional annual revenue by 2025, primarily through subscription services and enterprise licensing. This revenue potential attracts significant investment in AI infrastructure and talent, creating job opportunities and consulting demand.

For context, OpenAI's ChatGPT Plus subscription model generates an estimated $200+ million annually at $20 per month per user. The timing is particularly advantageous for entrepreneurs. Meta's brand recognition and technical capabilities mean Muse Spark will likely achieve faster mainstream adoption than previous AI models, creating a larger potential customer base for related services and products.
