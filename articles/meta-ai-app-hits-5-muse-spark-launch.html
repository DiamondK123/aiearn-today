Meta AI App Hits #5 After Muse Spark Launch

Meta AI climbed from #57 to #5 on App Store following new proprietary model launch. Shows strong user adoption of Meta's first closed-source AI assistant.

By AIEarn Editorial2026-04-11TechCrunch

The AI app landscape just witnessed a seismic shift. Meta's AI assistant app rocketed from position #57 to #5 on the App Store within days of launching Muse Spark, the company's first proprietary closed-source AI model. This dramatic climb signals more than just marketing buzz—it represents a pivotal moment for entrepreneurs and professionals looking to make money with AI tools as mainstream adoption accelerates.

This surge puts Meta AI ahead of established players and demonstrates that users are hungry for AI assistants that deliver tangible results. For the AI-savvy community at AIEarn.today, this development opens new doors for monetization strategies and business opportunities that weren't viable just months ago.

The Muse Spark Advantage: What's Different This Time

Meta's decision to develop Muse Spark as a closed-source model marks a strategic departure from the open-source approach that dominated their previous AI initiatives. Unlike their earlier Llama models, Muse Spark is designed specifically for consumer applications, with enhanced reasoning capabilities and faster response times that directly impact user experience.

The model's architecture focuses on practical tasks that everyday users actually need—from content creation and business planning to personal productivity optimization. Early reports suggest Muse Spark excels at generating marketing copy, analyzing business data, and providing strategic insights that previously required expensive consulting services.

What makes this particularly relevant for AI entrepreneurs is the tool's integration capabilities. Meta AI now seamlessly connects with Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp Business accounts, creating unprecedented opportunities for social media monetization and automated customer service solutions.

Three Proven Ways to Monetize Meta AI's New Capabilities

1. Launch an AI-Powered Content Agency

With Meta AI's enhanced content generation abilities, you can establish a specialized agency serving small businesses struggling with social media presence. The tool's integration with Meta's advertising platforms means you can offer end-to-end services—from content creation to ad optimization—without hiring additional staff.

Earning potential: Freelance content creators using AI assistance report charging $75-150 per hour for social media management, compared to $25-50 without AI tools. A single client package including content strategy, post creation, and performance analysis can command $2,000-5,000 monthly retainers.

2. Develop Custom Business Intelligence Services

Muse Spark's analytical capabilities allow you to offer data interpretation services to businesses drowning in metrics but lacking insights. Many companies spend thousands on analytics tools but struggle to translate data into actionable strategies.

Create standardized reporting templates using Meta AI to analyze customer behavior, sales trends, and market opportunities. Local restaurants, retail stores, and service providers will pay premium rates for clear, actionable business insights delivered weekly or monthly.

3. Scale Customer Service Operations

The WhatsApp Business integration creates opportunities to offer 24/7 customer service solutions for e-commerce businesses and service providers. Instead of hiring customer service representatives at $15-20 per hour, businesses can pay you $500-1,500 monthly for AI-powered support that handles routine inquiries, appointment scheduling, and order tracking.

The key is positioning this as a premium service that improves customer satisfaction while reducing operational costs—a win-win that justifies higher pricing.

Market Implications and Strategic Positioning

Meta AI's rapid ascent to #5 signals that mainstream users are moving beyond experimental AI usage toward practical application. This shift creates a narrowing window for early adopters to establish market position before competition intensifies.

The closed-source nature of Muse Spark also means fewer people will have access to its full capabilities compared to open-source alternatives. This scarcity creates value for those who master the platform early and can offer specialized services that others cannot easily replicate.

Industry analysts predict that AI assistant apps generating over $1 billion in revenue by 2025 will primarily be those with strong ecosystem integration—exactly what Meta AI now offers with its social media platform connections.

Getting Started: Your Action Plan

Begin by downloading Meta AI and spending 2-3 hours daily exploring its capabilities across different business scenarios. Document which tasks it handles exceptionally well and identify gaps where human oversight adds value.

Focus on building expertise in one vertical initially—whether that's content creation, data analysis, or customer service—before expanding. The businesses most likely to pay premium rates are those that understand their pain points but lack the technical expertise to implement AI solutions independently.

Consider the subscription economics: if Meta AI helps you generate an additional $1,000 monthly in client revenue, any reasonable subscription fee becomes insignificant compared to the value creation potential.

The Meta AI surge represents more than just another app ranking—it's a clear signal that AI monetization opportunities are shifting from experimental to mainstream.
